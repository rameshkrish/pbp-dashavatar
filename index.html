The Dasa (Ten) Avatars of Lord Vishnu

The story of Vishnu's avatars is a core part of Hindu mythology.

While everyone generally agrees on ten avatars, their stories and sometimes even the order can change depending on where you are in India.

Northern and Southern Indian interpretations often highlight the way ancient stories adapt to folktales, local cultures and regional beliefs across the Indian sub-continent.

Hindu texts describe four yugas in a Yuga Cycle (4,320,000 years), with each yuga's length decreasing in proportions of 4:3:2:1.
Satya Yuga (Age of Truth) lasts longest with perfect dharma, followed by Treta Yuga where virtue begins declining,
then Dvapara Yuga with further moral decay, and finally Kali Yuga (current age) - the shortest and darkest period.

After Kali Yuga ends, the cycle repeats with a new Satya Yuga, continuing eternally.

Matsya

(Fish)

Vishnu appears as a giant fish with a horn to lead the last people on a boat and save them from a great flood

Satya Yuga

Kurma

(Tortoise)

Vishnu appears as a tortoise to support Mount Mandara during the churning of the ocean for divine nectar

Satya Yuga

Varaha

(Boar)

Vishnu appears as a a boar to rescue mother-earth from the demon Hiranyaksha

Satya Yuga

Narsimha

(Lion-man)

Vishnu appears as a half-man, half-lion to defeat the demon Hiranyakashipu and save his disciple, Prahalad

Satya Yuga

Vamana

(Dwarf)

Vishnu appears as a dwarf to humble the benevolent but arrogant King Mahabali

Treta Yuga

Parshurama

(Warrior)

Vishnu appears as a axe-wielding warrior to rid the world of oppressive Kshatriyas

Treta Yuga

Rama

(Ideal King)

Vishnu appears a noble prince to exemplify righteousness and defeat the demon king Ravana

Treta Yuga

Krishna

(Divine Cowherd)

Vishnu appears as a cowherd who taught the Bhagavad Gita and guided the Pandavas to victory in Mahabharata

Dvapra Yuga

Buddha

(Enlightend Prince)

Vishnu appears as Siddhartha Gautama to teach the path of non-violence and enlightenment

Kali Yuga

Kalki

(Destroyer)

Vishnu is yet to appear on a white horse to destroy evil and restore dharma at the end of the current yuga
